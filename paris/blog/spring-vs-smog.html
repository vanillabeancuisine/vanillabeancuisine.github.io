Spring vs. Smog

Previous
Next
List

Whispers of spring here are everywhere. Every time I go out for a jog, I see a patch of flowers that I hadn’t noticed before (hello forsythias!), or count a few more cherry blossoms opening on the patches of trees interspersed along the Champs de Mars. (Who knew there would be so many flowers that remind me of springtime back home in DC?!) On the few sunny afternoons we’ve had in the past few weeks, the parks and gardens have been packed with people piquenique-ing or just lounging on the grass (despite the numerous signs prohibiting anyone from disturbing the grass during the winter rest time from October through mid-April—seriously Paris?!). But those afternoons have been few and far between, and I get the sense that we are all waiting somewhat impatiently for that legendary Paris spring to make its appearance. 

Overall it’s been overcast and just blah—not freezing, but still cold, and no sun to warm you. Oh, and last Wednesday, Paris had the highest air pollution in the world. Turns out those overcast skies were actually smog. Apparently it’s been so bad that the mayor made public transportation free for a few days and banned cars with license plates that ended in odd numbers from the road in an attempt to improve the air quality. All of this I figured out Sunday after googling to find out why the metro was free, and after having gone for a nice 3.5 mile run. Whoops. I really need to do a better job of tracking down some English news sources here… 

Today we finally have some rain, which is supposed to clear out the air somewhat, so at least it’s good for something. And in the meantime, I’m going to send my mind back to a week or so ago, when the temperature hit the upper 60s, and we and everyone else in Paris packed up our baguettes, meats, and cheeses and went to a park for lunch. 

Our choice was the Tuileries, where we saw the beautiful paintings at Musée de L’Orangerie before lounging in those awesome green chairs by the fountain. Monet’s water lilies may be cliché, but personally, I adore them, and I loved showing the girls how the paintings change when you are close up versus further back. Our first lesson in Impressionism!

No leaves on the trees yet, but the girls did a good job of providing some color to the scene. At one point they were running next to each other, Piper with her red ladybug backpack, and Ruby with her blue puppy backpack—they might have stood out just a little.

Back at home, I have decided that if nothing else, I’m declaring it salad season. There are still lovely greens to be had, and at my Tuesday market, one vendor was steaming and selling the most gigantic beets I have ever seen. I don’t need a stronger invitation than that to make a beet salad. For the dressing, I have a go-to vinaigrette recipe that I use on everything. Make a batch for yourself and you’ll see why. 


Easy Peasy Lemon Squeezy Vinaigrette

- lemon
- dijon mustard (and/or whole-grain mustard)
- olive oil
- kosher salt & freshly ground pepper

Halve your lemon and squeeze both halves into a big bowl. Scoop out any seeds that fell in. Add a couple of teaspoons of mustard. I like to combine a little dijon with some whole-grain, but you can do as you like, or as you have in the fridge. Whisk. Then, as you continue whisking, drizzle in your olive oil. The traditional ratio is 3 parts oil to 1 part lemon (or vinegar), but I like mine closer to 2:1. Really though, I just whisk in a bunch of oil, taste it, and then decide whether to add more. Once it’s not too sharp for you, whisk in some salt and pepper. Then put it in a jar in your fridge and shake it up when you need it.

My husband and mom can tell you that I once made the statement, “and I’m making my own salad dressing because I’m a chef, dammit!” during a cooking demo, but really, everyone should do it! It’s easy, delicious, and so much better for you than the store-bought options.  

Then, you just throw together some greens, chunks of beet, and some goat cheese, drizzle it with your vinaigrette, and there’s lunch. Oh, and if you are feeling fancy, there is nothing better than some candied nuts on top. I love making my own, though they rarely last long enough to be just sitting around when I happen to make a beet salad. Never fear, Trader Joe’s makes some awesome candied walnuts and pecans that I do stash in my pantry for just such emergencies! 

Bon courage, mes amis, spring is coming!
